A Tribute to Jordan B Peterson

The New Father Figure

Jordan B Peterson is a clinical psychologist and professor of psychology at the University of Toronto in Canada. His focus resides on two main areas: the psychology of religious and ideological beliefs, and human personality.

A Timeline of Events

  • 1962 - was born in Edmonton, Alberta.
  • 1975 - the school librarian introduced him to authors like Orwell, Solzhenitzyn, and Rand.
  • 1982 - received a BA in political science.
  • 1984 - after taking a year off to visit europe, where he became interested in mankind's capacity for evil during the 20th century (especially the cold war), he completed a BA in psychology.
  • 1993 - after receiving a Ph.D. in clinical psychology, he began working at Harvard as an associate professor.
  • 1997 - returned to Canada to be a professor of psychology at U of Toronto.
  • 2004 - a thirteen-part TV series was produced based on his book Maps of Meaning: The Architecture of Belief, published five years previously.
  • 2014 - Peterson began to upload his university lectures to YouTube.
  • 2015 - the Self Authoring program, a writing exercise designed by a group of psychologists (including Peterson) to help the user create their ideal future and act toward it, is released.
  • 2016 - a video of Peterson and a group of students debating goes relatively viral, receiving 1.8 million views in one month. This is where I first heard of Peterson. Following this video, he then appeared on the Joe Rogan Experience podcast, where he made his aversion to Bill C-16 widely known. This was due to it's parallels with what he had found when studying evil and the cold war.
  • 2017 - due to his increasing popularity, more of the public find out about and watch his lectures. Peterson's lessons on cleaning your room, growing up, facing the unknown and slaying the dragon resonate with his viewers, especially with young men, he notes. For me, and for others, the teachings of his lectures and the guidance of the Self Authoring program have been life-saving.

If you want to learn more about Jordan Peterson, check out his wikipedia page, or watch one of his lectures.
